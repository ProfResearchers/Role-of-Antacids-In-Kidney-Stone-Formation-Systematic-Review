# Our Synthesis Plan

Based on the **SWiM (Synthesis Without Meta-analysis)** guidelines and the **ESRC Narrative Synthesis framework**, here is a detailed plan to synthesize your extracted data.

### **Phase 1: Grouping and Clustering the Studies**

You cannot synthesize all 47 studies in a single narrative flow. You must group them logically to tell a coherent story. Based on your PROSPERO protocol and the nature of antacids, use the following **four clusters**:

#### **Cluster 1: The "Silica Stone" Phenomenon (Iatrogenic Stones)**

-   **Intervention:** Magnesium Trisilicate / Silicate-containing antacids.
    
-   **Mechanism:** Reaction with gastric acid $\rightarrow$Silica gel formation $\rightarrow$Renal excretion $\rightarrow$Stone nucleation.
    
-   **Study Types:** Mostly Case Reports and Case Series (e.g., *Herman, Lagergren, Joekes*).
    
-   **Synthesis Goal:** Establish causality between long-term antacid use and this rare stone type.
    

#### **Cluster 2: Calcium & Milk-Alkali Syndrome (Hypercalcemia)**

-   **Intervention:** Calcium Carbonate (often with milk/vitamin D).
    
-   **Mechanism:** Absorptive hypercalciuria + Alkalosis $\rightarrow$Ca precipitation.
    
-   **Study Types:** Case reports (*Aloia, Kolnick*), Cohorts, some trials.
    
-   **Synthesis Goal:** Define the threshold of "harm" (dose/duration) and risk factors (pregnancy, renal failure).
    

#### **Cluster 3: Magnesium & Citrate (Preventive vs. Causative)**

-   **Intervention:** Magnesium Oxide/Hydroxide, Potassium/Sodium Citrate (often used as alkalinizing agents).
    
-   **Mechanism:**
    
    -   *Prevention:* Mg competes with Ca; Citrate binds Ca.
        
    -   *Causation:* Struvite stones in alkaline urine (high pH).
        
-   **Study Types:** RCTs (*Ettinger, Johansson*), Rat models (*Ogawa*).
    
-   **Synthesis Goal:** Compare efficacy in preventing CaOx stones vs. risk of forming Phosphate stones.
    

#### **Cluster 4: Mechanism of Action (Animal/In Vitro)**

-   **Population:** Rats, Pigs, In-vitro cells.
    
-   **Outcomes:** Urinary pH changes, Crystal morphology, biochemical pathways.
    
-   **Synthesis Goal:** Provide the biological plausibility for the clinical findings in Clusters 1-3.

### **Phase 2: Data Transformation and Visual Presentation**

Since you cannot pool data mathematically, you must use visual tools to show patterns.

**2.1. Create a "Harvest Plot" (Super-Matrix)** Create a visual matrix for each cluster.

-   **Rows:** Outcomes (e.g., Stone Recurrence, Urinary pH, Ca Excretion).
    
-   **Columns:** Study ID.
    
-   **Cell Content:** Use symbols to indicate direction of effect:
    
    -   (⬆) Significant increase (Harm).
        
    -   (⬇) Significant decrease (Benefit).
        
    -   (↔) No significant change.
        
-   **Color Coding:** Color the cell based on the **Risk of Bias (RoB)** rating you completed (Green=Low, Yellow=Moderate, Red=High).
    

**2.2. Effect Direction Plot**

-   Group studies by intervention (e.g., Magnesium Hydroxide).
    
-   Calculate the percentage of studies showing a "harmful" effect (stone formation) vs. "protective" effect.
    
-   *Example:* "Of 12 studies on Mg Hydroxide, 8 showed increased urinary Mg (protective), but only 3 showed reduced stone recurrence."

### **Phase 3: The Narrative Writing (The Synthesis)**

Write your results section using the following structure. This ensures you move beyond just describing study-by-study (a common mistake) and actually *synthesize* the evidence.

#### **Section A: Biochemical Mechanisms (The "Why")**

-   Synthesize the **Animal/In-Vitro data**.
    
-   Explain how different antacids alter urinary pH.
    
-   *Key question to answer:* Does increasing urinary pH via Bicarbonate/Citrate universally prevent stones, or does it shift the risk from Uric Acid/Cystine stones to Calcium Phosphate stones? (Reference the *Pig studies* here).
    

#### **Section B: Clinical Evidence of Harm (The "Risk")**

-   **Silica Stones:** Synthesize the case reports. Don't list them one by one. instead, write: *"Across [X] case reports spanning 1960-2000, long-term Magnesium Trisilicate use was consistently linked to radiolucent silica calculi..."*
    
-   **Milk-Alkali:** Synthesize the Calcium Carbonate studies. Highlight the interaction between pregnancy, renal function, and antacid dose.
    

#### **Section C: Clinical Evidence of Efficacy (The "Prevention")**

-   Focus on the RCTs here.
    
-   Compare **Magnesium vs. Placebo**. (Discuss the conflict between *Ettinger* [no effect] and *Johansson* [positive effect]).
    
-   Compare **Citrate vs. Placebo**.
    
-   *Crucial Step:* Use your Quality Appraisal here. If results conflict, give more weight to the Low RoB studies in your narrative.

### **Phase 4: Exploring Heterogeneity (Moderator Analysis)**

You must explain *why* the 47 studies have different results. Write a specific section discussing:

1.  **Dosing Duration:** Do stones only form after years of use (Silica) vs. acute changes (Milk-Alkali)?
    
2.  **Base Formulation:** Compare *Magnesium Oxide* (poorly absorbed) vs. *Magnesium Citrate* (better absorbed). Does this explain why some Mg studies failed?
    
3.  **Baseline Pathology:** Did the study include healthy people (low risk) or recurrent stone formers (high risk)?
    
4.  **"Splitting" Bias:** (Referencing *Thomson et al.* from your files) Did studies fail to find significance because they split patients into too many subgroups (e.g., hypercalciuric vs. normocalciuric)?
    

---

### **Phase 5: Assessing Certainty (The GRADE Approach)**

Even without a meta-analysis, you must assign a "certainty" level to your conclusions using **Narrative GRADE**:

-   **High Certainty:** For the link between Ca-Carbonate and Milk-Alkali syndrome (strong signal, biological plausibility).
    
-   **Moderate Certainty:** For the ability of Citrate to alkalize urine.
    
-   **Low/Very Low Certainty:** For the ability of Magnesium supplements to *prevent* stone recurrence (due to conflicting RCT results and imprecision).
    

### **Checklist for your Final Report:**

1.  **PRISMA Flowchart:** Show the 47 studies included.
    
2.  **Study Characteristics Table:** Columns for Author, Design, Population, Intervention, Outcome, RoB Rating.
    
3.  **Synthesis text:** Structured by Antacid Type (not by study).
    
4.  **Limitations:** Discuss the high number of case reports and animal studies vs. paucity of modern RCTs on antacids.
    
5.  **Conclusion:** Clear clinical guidance on which antacids pose a stone risk (Silicates, Calcium Carbonate in high doses) and which might be protective (Citrate).
